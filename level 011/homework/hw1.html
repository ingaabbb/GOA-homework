Ice skating

Ice skating is the self- propulsion and gliding of a person across an ice surface, using metal-bladed ice skates.

History

Research suggests that the earliest ice skating happened in southern Finland more than 4,000 years ago.

Figure skating

The first instructional book concerning ice skating was published in London in 1772.

Physical mechanics of skating

A skate can glide over ice because there is a layer of ice molecules on the surface that are not as tightly bound as the molecules of the mass of ice beneath.
